Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good typing speed?
The average typing speed is around 40 WPM. A good typing speed is 60–80 WPM. Professional typists typically achieve 80–100+ WPM. Competitive typists can exceed 150 WPM.
How can I improve my typing speed?
Practice regularly using TypeFlow's various modes. Focus on accuracy first, then speed. Use proper finger placement (home row technique). Practice with different content types including coding snippets. Take the 60-second test daily to track your progress.
What is WPM in typing?
WPM stands for Words Per Minute. It measures typing speed by counting the number of words typed correctly in one minute. A "word" is standardized as 5 characters. Raw WPM includes errors, while adjusted WPM (net WPM) subtracts penalties for mistakes.
What is the difference between WPM and Raw WPM?
WPM (net WPM) counts only correctly typed characters, giving you your effective speed. Raw WPM counts all typed characters including errors. The difference between the two indicates how many errors you're making.
Why practice coding typing?
Developers spend significant time typing code, which includes special characters like brackets, semicolons, and operators. Practicing with real code snippets helps you type faster in your daily work and reduces the cognitive load of finding special keys.

Complete Guide to Improving Your Typing Speed

Whether you're a beginner or looking to push past a plateau, this guide covers the fundamentals and advanced techniques for faster, more accurate typing.

1. Master the Home Row

Place your fingers on A-S-D-F (left hand) and J-K-L-; (right hand). Your thumbs rest on the space bar. This is the foundation of touch typing — every finger has designated keys to reach.

2. Focus on Accuracy First

Speed without accuracy is counterproductive. Aim for 95%+ accuracy before pushing for higher WPM. Errors require backspace corrections that waste more time than typing slowly and correctly.

3. Practice Consistently

15 minutes of daily practice is more effective than occasional hour-long sessions. Use TypeFlow's timer mode for structured practice sessions.

  • Week 1-2: Focus on accuracy with easy words at a comfortable pace
  • Week 3-4: Increase difficulty to medium, maintain 95% accuracy
  • Week 5-8: Try timed tests and push your speed while keeping accuracy above 90%
  • Ongoing: Add coding practice and quotes for varied typing patterns

4. Don't Look at the Keyboard

This is the hardest habit to break, but the most impactful. Cover your keyboard if needed. Your muscle memory will develop faster when your eyes stay on the screen.

5. Coding Practice for Developers

TypeFlow's code mode lets you practice with real JavaScript, Python, TypeScript, and SQL snippets. This builds muscle memory for brackets, semicolons, colons, and other special characters that are common in programming.

6. Track Your Progress

TypeFlow saves your test history locally in your browser. Review your history to identify trends — are you improving? Is your accuracy consistent? Use this data to adjust your practice strategy.
